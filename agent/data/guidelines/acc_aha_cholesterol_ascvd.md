## Statin intensity for secondary prevention

Patients with clinical atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD), including a history of
myocardial infarction, stable or unstable angina, or coronary revascularization, should be treated
with high-intensity statin therapy (e.g., atorvastatin 40-80mg or rosuvastatin 20-40mg) unless
contraindicated, with a goal of reducing LDL cholesterol by 50% or more from baseline.

## LDL targets and add-on therapy

For patients with established ASCVD already on maximally tolerated statin therapy whose LDL
cholesterol remains 70 mg/dL or higher, adding ezetimibe is reasonable; a PCSK9 inhibitor may be
considered if LDL remains elevated despite statin plus ezetimibe, particularly in very-high-risk
patients (multiple prior ASCVD events or ASCVD plus additional high-risk conditions).

## Antiplatelet therapy

Low-dose aspirin (75-100mg daily) is recommended for secondary prevention in patients with
established coronary artery disease absent a contraindication, alongside statin therapy and risk
factor modification (blood pressure control, smoking cessation, diabetes management).

## Concomitant risk factor management

Patients with coronary artery disease and coexisting but unrelated conditions (e.g., osteoarthritis)
should have their cardiac risk-reduction regimen prioritized in acute evaluation of new or
worsening cardiac symptoms; unrelated chronic conditions should be noted but not allowed to
distract from time-sensitive cardiac risk assessment.
