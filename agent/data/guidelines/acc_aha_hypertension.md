## Blood pressure categories

Normal blood pressure is defined as systolic below 120 mmHg and diastolic below 80 mmHg. Elevated
blood pressure is systolic 120-129 with diastolic below 80. Stage 1 hypertension is systolic
130-139 or diastolic 80-89. Stage 2 hypertension is systolic 140 or higher, or diastolic 90 or
higher.

## When to initiate pharmacotherapy

Antihypertensive medication is recommended for patients with stage 1 hypertension who also have
clinical cardiovascular disease, a 10-year atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease risk of 10% or
higher, diabetes, or chronic kidney disease. For patients with stage 2 hypertension, pharmacologic
therapy is recommended regardless of estimated cardiovascular risk, generally started as a
two-drug combination.

## First-line agent classes

First-line drug classes for most patients include thiazide diuretics, calcium channel blockers,
and ACE inhibitors or ARBs. ACE inhibitors and ARBs are preferred first-line agents in patients
with diabetes and albuminuria, or with heart failure with reduced ejection fraction, given
additional renal and cardiac protective effects beyond blood pressure lowering alone.

## Treatment target

A blood pressure target below 130/80 mmHg is recommended for most adults with confirmed
hypertension, including those with diabetes or chronic kidney disease.
