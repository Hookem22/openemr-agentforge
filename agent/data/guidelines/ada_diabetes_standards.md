## Diagnostic thresholds

A hemoglobin A1c of 6.5% or higher, a fasting plasma glucose of 126 mg/dL or higher, or a 2-hour
plasma glucose of 200 mg/dL or higher during an oral glucose tolerance test are each independently
sufficient to diagnose diabetes, and should be confirmed with repeat testing unless the patient
presents with unambiguous hyperglycemia and a random glucose of 200 mg/dL or higher. An A1c between
5.7% and 6.4% is classified as prediabetes.

## Glycemic targets

A general A1c target of below 7% is reasonable for most nonpregnant adults, but targets should be
individualized: a tighter target (below 6.5%) may be appropriate for patients with short disease
duration and no significant cardiovascular disease, while a looser target (below 8%) is reasonable
for patients with limited life expectancy, extensive comorbidity, or a history of severe
hypoglycemia.

## First-line and add-on pharmacotherapy

Metformin remains first-line therapy for most patients with type 2 diabetes absent
contraindications. For patients with established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, heart
failure, or chronic kidney disease, a GLP-1 receptor agonist or SGLT2 inhibitor with demonstrated
benefit should be added independent of A1c control or metformin use, given their proven
cardiovascular and renal risk-reduction benefits.

## Monitoring cadence

A1c should be checked at least twice yearly in patients meeting treatment goals with stable
glycemic control, and quarterly in patients not meeting goals or with a recent therapy change.
