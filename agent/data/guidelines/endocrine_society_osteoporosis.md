## Screening recommendations

Bone mineral density screening with DXA is recommended for all women aged 65 and older, and for
postmenopausal women under 65 with additional risk factors (low body weight, prior fracture, family
history of hip fracture, or long-term glucocorticoid use). A T-score of -2.5 or lower at the hip or
spine establishes a diagnosis of osteoporosis; a T-score between -1.0 and -2.5 indicates osteopenia
(low bone mass).

## First-line pharmacologic therapy

Oral bisphosphonates (alendronate, risedronate) are first-line therapy for most postmenopausal
women with osteoporosis, given their fracture-risk reduction, favorable long-term safety profile,
and low cost. Weekly oral dosing (e.g., alendronate 70mg once weekly) is standard.

## Calcium and vitamin D

Adequate calcium (1000-1200mg/day, diet plus supplementation as needed) and vitamin D (800-1000
IU/day) intake should be ensured in all patients on bisphosphonate therapy, as adequate vitamin D
status (25-OH vitamin D generally 30 ng/mL or higher) is necessary for bisphosphonate efficacy; a
low vitamin D level should be corrected before or concurrent with starting therapy.

## Monitoring during treatment

Routine annual monitoring visits for patients on stable bisphosphonate therapy without new
complaints should confirm continued medication adherence, reassess fracture risk factors, and
check vitamin D and basic metabolic status periodically rather than requiring repeat DXA more
often than every 1-2 years absent a new clinical concern.
