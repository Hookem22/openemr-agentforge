## Blood pressure screening

The USPSTF recommends screening for hypertension in adults 18 and older with office blood pressure
measurement. For adults with a normal reading, rescreening every 3-5 years is reasonable; annual
screening is recommended for adults aged 40 or older or those at increased risk (elevated blood
pressure, obesity).

## Lipid and cardiovascular risk screening

Screening for lipid disorders is recommended in adults aged 40-75 as part of overall
cardiovascular risk assessment. Statin use for primary prevention should be informed by a
calculated 10-year atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease risk in this age group.

## Diabetes screening

Screening for prediabetes and type 2 diabetes is recommended in adults aged 35-70 who have
overweight or obesity; testing should be repeated every 3 years if the initial result is normal.

## Preventive visit cadence for average-risk adults

For an asymptomatic adult with no active complaints and no significant risk factors, an annual
wellness visit focused on preventive screening, immunization review, and risk-factor counseling is
reasonable, without additional diagnostic testing beyond the age- and risk-based screenings above.
