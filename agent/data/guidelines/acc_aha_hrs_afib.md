## Stroke risk stratification

The CHA2DS2-VASc score is the recommended tool for estimating stroke risk in patients with
nonvalvular atrial fibrillation, assigning points for congestive heart failure, hypertension, age
75 or older (2 points), diabetes, prior stroke or TIA (2 points), vascular disease, age 65-74, and
female sex.

## Anticoagulation thresholds

Oral anticoagulation is recommended for patients with a CHA2DS2-VASc score of 2 or higher in men or
3 or higher in women. For a score of 1 in men or 2 in women, anticoagulation may be considered
based on individual risk-benefit assessment. Direct oral anticoagulants are preferred over warfarin
in most eligible patients with nonvalvular atrial fibrillation due to comparable or superior
efficacy with lower rates of intracranial hemorrhage.

## Rate versus rhythm control

Either rate control or rhythm control is a reasonable initial strategy for most patients with new
atrial fibrillation; rhythm control (antiarrhythmic therapy or catheter ablation) is preferred
for patients who remain symptomatic despite adequate rate control, or for younger patients with new-
onset atrial fibrillation and few comorbidities, where earlier rhythm control has been associated
with better long-term outcomes.

## New-onset atrial fibrillation

A new diagnosis of atrial fibrillation should prompt evaluation for reversible or contributing
causes (thyroid dysfunction, structural heart disease, sleep apnea) alongside initiation of
appropriate stroke risk-reduction therapy.
